Thinking as a chartered accountant running a Nigerian port clearing firm, here is a much deeper list — organized by accounting discipline:

Receivables & Cash Flow
1. Invoice & Payment Tracking
Every container cleared is a service rendered. The app currently tracks your costs but not your income collection. Add: raise an invoice from clearing charges, record client payments (full/partial), track outstanding balances, and flag overdue invoices. This turns the app into a proper debtors ledger.

2. Receivables Aging Report
Group unpaid invoices by age — 0–30 days, 31–60, 61–90, 90+ days overdue. Standard accounting tool that shows exactly who owes money and for how long. Essential for cash flow management.

3. Client Statement of Account
A formal monthly statement per client showing all containers, invoices raised, payments received, and closing balance — the kind you send to a client as a formal account summary.

4. Cash Flow Forecast
Based on containers currently in progress and their expected completion dates, project expected income for the next 30, 60, and 90 days. Also project expected cost outflows. Shows whether you'll have a cash deficit before it happens.

Cost Control & Compliance
5. Budget vs Actual per Container
Before clearing begins, enter expected costs for each category. When actual costs are entered, the system flags any category that exceeds its budget, with a percentage variance. This is standard cost accounting.

6. VAT Register
In Nigeria, VAT (7.5%) applies to many clearing-related services. Track which line items attract VAT, auto-calculate the VAT component, and produce a VAT register that feeds into your FIRS filing. Separates VAT collected from revenue earned.

7. Withholding Tax (WHT) Tracker
Track WHT deducted from payments to service providers (shipping companies, agents, etc.) as required under Nigerian tax law. Show cumulative WHT obligations for remittance to FIRS.

8. Expense Anomaly Detection
If terminal charges for a 20ft container suddenly cost 3× the historical average, flag it automatically. An alert that says "Terminal charges are 280% above your average for this container size" catches billing errors and fraud early.

9. Cost Cap / Margin Protection Alerts
Set a minimum acceptable gross margin (e.g. 15%). When entering charges, the system calculates in real time whether that container will fall below the margin threshold — and alerts the operator before it does.

Profitability Intelligence
10. Break-Even Calculator
For any container, calculate the minimum clearing charge needed to cover all costs and break even. Shows instantly whether a quoted price to the client is viable.

11. Profitability Ranking
Rank containers by gross margin %, rank clients by total profit contributed, rank vessel/shipping lines by average profitability of their cargo. Answers "who are our most valuable clients?" and "which type of cargo makes us the most money?"

12. Year-to-Date vs Prior Year Comparison
Compare this month's and this year's revenue, costs, and profit against the same period last year. Fundamental for understanding whether the business is growing or shrinking.

13. Cost per TEU Benchmarking
Calculate your average cost to clear a 20ft container vs a 40ft container. Track this over time. If your cost per TEU is rising while margins are flat, something in your operations is inefficient.

Demurrage & Port-Specific Intelligence
14. Demurrage Calculator & Early Warning
Terminal demurrage at Apapa/Tin-Can accumulates daily after free days expire — this is one of the largest uncontrolled costs in Nigerian clearing. Track: vessel arrival date, number of free days given, daily demurrage rate, and calculate exactly how much is accumulating per day with a countdown alert. When free days are about to expire, send a prominent warning.

15. Clearing Turnaround Time Analytics
Track exactly how many days each container spends at each workflow stage. Identify which stage causes the most delay across all containers. Over time this builds a picture of your operational efficiency — and where you're losing money to time.

Creditors & Payables
16. Creditors/Payables Ledger
Track amounts owed to shipping companies, terminal operators, customs agents, haulage companies, etc. Record payment dates. See total outstanding payables at any time. Complements the receivables side to give a complete picture of your financial position.

Exchange Rate & Forex
17. Multi-Currency Cost Entry with Exchange Rate Lock
Many port charges — shipping freight, FOB, CIF values — are billed in USD. Allow each line item to be entered in USD with the exchange rate on the date of payment, and convert to NGN automatically. Lock the rate so historical records don't change when the naira moves. Gives accurate naira P&L even when costs are in foreign currency.

Reporting
18. Management Accounts Pack
A one-click report that produces a proper management accounts pack: P&L for the period, top 10 containers by profit, top 10 by loss, client profitability table, cash position, and outstanding receivables. The kind of document you'd present to a board or bank.

19. Container Profitability Heatmap
A visual grid showing each container as a colored tile — green (high margin), amber (low margin), red (loss) — giving an instant overview of the financial health of your entire portfolio at a glance.
